1. PROJECT BACKGROUND & OBJECTIVE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The objective of this business intelligence project is to transform decentralized, 
heterogeneous supply chain logs into a centralized strategic asset. By analyzing 
historical fleet telemetry, fuel purchasing ledgers, maintenance records, and safety 
incidents tracking 122M miles and 85K dispatches, this dashboard highlights clear 
bottom-line cost leaks, driver tracking performance, and network delivery bottlenecks.

The final architecture was consolidated into a streamlined 4-Page configuration 
to maximize executive screen real estate and establish intuitive analytical drill-downs.


2. ARCHITECTURAL DATA DICTIONARY & RELATIONSHIPS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The data model uses a performance-optimized Star Schema built from 14 source files, 
anchored to a central temporal dimension (Dim_Date) and linked across four fact tables:

A. TRIPS LOGS FACT
   - actual_distance_miles: Physical mileage traversed per dispatch.
   - fuel_gallons_used: Total diesel gallons consumed during transit.
   - idle_time_hours: Non-productive engine runtime hours.
   - load_id / trip_id: Relational transactional keys.
   - trip_status: Binary delivery tracking metric (True = On-Time, False = Delayed).

B. FUEL PURCHASES FACT
   - purchase_date: Transaction timestamp linked to Dim_Date[Date].
   - total_cost: Raw cash layout for individual fueling events.
   - gallons: Volume pumped during the purchase transaction.
   - location_city / location_state: Geographic transaction tags.

C. MAINTENANCE LOGS FACT
   - downtime_hours: Hours an asset was non-operational due to service.
   - maintenance_cost: Invoiced cost for parts and mechanical labor.
   - asset_id (truck_id): Relational key linking back to the TRUCKS dimension.

D. SAFETY EVENTS FACT
   - incident_id: Unique asset exception key.
   - incident_type: Categorized risk event (Accident, DOT Violation, Moving Violation).
   - vehicle_damage_cost / cargo_damage_cost: Hard physical financial liabilities.
   - preventable_flag / at_fault_flag: Risk governance qualifiers (1 = Yes, 0 = No).


3. KEY CORE DAX METRICS & ASSUMPTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
To maintain extreme mathematical accuracy across deep aggregations, the following 
operational metrics were built using advanced DAX modeling best practices:

* Gross Total Revenue 
  Formula: [Base Linehaul Revenue] + [Total Accessorial Revenue]
  Logic: Reference measures are nested for cleaner maintenance. If underlying linehaul 
  or extra accessorial structures change, the master metric updates automatically.

* Average Fleet MPG
  Formula: DIVIDE(SUM('Trips'[actual_distance_miles]), SUM('Trips'[fuel_gallons_used]), 0)
  Assumption: Direct column averaging (AVERAGE(Trips[average_mpg])) distorts true system 
  performance. This measure divides total operational distance by total mass consumption 
  for a true fleet-wide average baseline (Project Benchmark: 6.45 MPG).

* Total Safety Incident Cost
  Formula: SUM('Safety Incidents'[vehicle_damage_cost]) + SUM('Safety Incidents'[cargo_damage_cost])
  Logic: Expresses the combined bottom-line risk liability by uniting physical equipment 
  damage with freight insurance claims.

* Idle Fuel Waste Cost
  Formula: SUM('Trips'[idle_time_hours]) * [Dynamic Avg Fuel Price]
  Assumption: Standard fleet baseline assumptions dictate that heavy-duty commercial 
  power units consume roughly 1.0 gallon of diesel fuel per hour of idling. This calculates 
  the exact financial waste incurred when trucks run engines while standing still.


4. DASHBOARD PAGE LOGIC & METRIC MATRIX
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

PAGE 1: EXECUTIVE FINANCIAL OVERVIEW
- Captures top-line growth ($298.62M Gross Revenue), expenditures ($101.32M), 
  and net profits ($197.30M).
- Isolates revenue capture by core market segments, highlighting that Dedicated 
  Contract Freight commands the largest share at 49.60% ($148.12M), followed by 
  Spot Market Dispatches (25.26%) and Corporate Contracts (25.14%).

PAGE 2: FLEET UTILIZATION & MAINTENANCE
- Audits $5.73M in total upkeep expenses across 72.23K total downtime hours.
- Incorporates horizontal rankings to isolate critical power unit outliers 
  (e.g., TRK00003 accruing $90K in maintenance costs).
- Categorizes repairs using a TreeMap to separate standard Preventive maintenance 
  from systemic failures in Brake, Tire, or Transmission assemblies.

PAGE 3: SUPPLY CHAIN LOGISTICS & ROUTE PROFITABILITY
- Pinpoints severe network delays totaling 260,607 aggregate detention hours.
- Leverages dual-axis visuals to map regional state revenues against lane distance, 
  identifying Oregon ($40M), California, and Washington as core financial corridors.
- Visualizes systemic delivery failures where delayed dispatches (55.67%) outpace 
  on-time performance (44.33%).

PAGE 4: FLEET EFFICIENCY & PERFORMANCE MATRIX
- Consolidates fuel spend ($95.59M) alongside risk management records ($2.65M 
  claims over 170 infractions).
- Features an executive driver leaderboard scorecard using a Matrix visualization. 
  By adding inline Sparklines (X-Axis: Month, Y-Axis: Score) and conditional gradient 
  formatting, managers can visually identify underperforming drivers and track 
  improvement over time.
